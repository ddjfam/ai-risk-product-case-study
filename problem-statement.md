# Problem Statement

## Background

Organizations adopting AI/ML solutions in regulated or risk-sensitive environments often face fragmented workflows, inconsistent intake processes, limited visibility, and manual coordination across multiple stakeholders. These issues slow delivery, create confusion, and make it harder to scale risk-aware product decisions.

In enterprise settings, AI-enabled products must do more than function well technically. They must also support governance, transparency, usability, and operational efficiency.

## Core Problem

Risk and governance teams frequently work across disconnected tools, duplicate reviews, unclear ownership, and inconsistent decision criteria. As a result:

- requests take too long to move through the workflow
- teams lack visibility into status, priorities, and dependencies
- stakeholder handoffs are inefficient
- product decisions are harder to trace and explain
- improvement opportunities are identified too late

## Why It Matters

When AI-related risk workflows are not structured clearly, organizations may experience:

- slower product delivery
- poor user experience for internal stakeholders
- reduced confidence in workflow outputs
- inconsistent prioritization of enhancements
- higher operational burden on risk, product, and operations teams

## Opportunity

There is an opportunity to create a more effective product experience that brings together:

- standardized intake and review workflows
- clearer ownership and decision paths
- better prioritization of work
- improved transparency into workflow status and outcomes
- stronger alignment between user needs, controls, and delivery goals

## Desired Outcome

The desired outcome is an AI-enabled risk workflow experience that is:

- easier for stakeholders to use
- more transparent across the review lifecycle
- better aligned to governance and operational needs
- more scalable as demand increases
- structured to support measurable product and process improvement

## Product Framing

This case study frames the opportunity as a product challenge, not just a process issue. The goal is to improve the end-to-end user and stakeholder experience by combining:

- product strategy
- workflow design
- prioritization discipline
- stakeholder alignment
- measurable success criteria

## Success Questions

A strong solution should help answer questions such as:

- How can intake, review, and decision workflows become more consistent?
- Where are the biggest friction points for users and stakeholders?
- Which features or workflow improvements create the highest impact?
- How should product decisions balance usability, controls, and efficiency?
- What metrics best indicate product and operational success?
